Kautz graph


The Kautz graph 𝒦KN+1 is a digraph (directed graph) of degree K and dimension N+1, which has (K+1)⁢KN vertices labeled by all possible strings s0⁢⋯⁢sN of length N+1 which are composed of characters si chosen from an alphabet A containing K+1 distinct symbols, subject to the condition that adjacent characters in the string cannot be equal (si≠si+1).

The Kautz graph 𝒦KN+1 has (K+1)⁢KN+1 edges

{(s0⁢s1⁢⋯⁢sN,s1⁢s2⁢⋯⁢sN⁢sN+1)|si∈A⁢si≠si+1}. (1)

It is natural to label each such edge of 𝒦KN+1 as s0⁢s1⁢⋯⁢sN+1, giving a one-to-one correspondence between edges of the Kautz graph 𝒦KN+1 and vertices of the Kautz graph 𝒦KN+2.

Properties:

∙ The diameter of the Kautz graph 𝒦KN is N
(For example, there is a path of length N from x to y achieved by the sequence of edges (x0⁢x1⁢⋯⁢xN,x1⁢⋯⁢xN⁢y0), …(xN⁢y0⁢⋯⁢yN-1,y0⁢⋯⁢yN) unless xN=y0 in which case there is a similar path of length N-1 beginning (x0⁢x1⁢⋯⁢xN-1⁢y0,x1⁢⋯⁢xN-1⁢y0⁢y1),….)

∙ Kautz graphs are closely related to de Bruijn graphs, which are defined similarly but without the condition si≠si+1, and with an alphabet of only K symbols for the degree K de Bruijn graph.

∙ For a fixed degree K and number of vertices V=(K+1)⁢KN, the Kautz graph has the smallest diameter of any possible directed graph with V vertices and degree K.

∙ All Kautz graphs have Eulerian cycles
(An Eulerian cycle is one which visits each edge exactly once– This result follows because Kautz graphs have in-degree equal to out-degree for each node)

∙ All Kautz graphs have a Hamiltonian cycle
(This result follows from the correspondence described above between edges of the Kautz graph 𝒦KN and vertices of the Kautz graph 𝒦KN+1; a Hamiltonian cycle on 𝒦KN+1 is given by an Eulerian cycle on 𝒦KN)

∙ A degree-k Kautz graph has k disjoint paths from any node x to any other node y.
