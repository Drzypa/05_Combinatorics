Kneser graphs


Let k,n be positive integers with k≤n. The Kneser graph Kn:k has as its vertex set the (nk) k-element subsets of {1,2,…,n}. Two vertices are adjacent if and only if they correspond to disjoint subsets.

The graph Kn:1 is the complete graph on n vertices. Another well-known Kneser graph is K5:2, better known as the Petersen graph, which is shown in figure 1. The Petersen graph is often a counterexample to graph-theoretical conjectures.
