expectation example


The contraharmonic mean of several positive numbers u1, u2, …, un is defined as

c:=u12+u22+…+un2u1+u2+…+un.

This has certain applications; one of them is by [1] the following.

If ⟨u1, u2, …, un⟩ is the distribution of the seats of n parties, s the total number of seats in the body of delegates (u1+u2+…+un=s), and one draws a random seat (with probability 1/s), then the size of the drawn delegate’s party has the expected value

u1s⋅u1+u2s⋅u2+…+uns⋅un=c.

References

  • 1 Caulier, Jean-François: The interpretation of the Laakso–Taagepera effective number of parties.  – Documents de travail du Centre d’Economie de la Sorbonne (2011.06).
